THE SARS PROTESTS IN NIGERIA

The Special anti-robbery squad (SARS) is a special police force in Nigeria that was made to combat internet fraud in Nigeria but that is not exactly what they are doing. There have been a lot of cases in Nigeria about the misconduct and abuse of power by SARS officials. The SARS official profile Nigerian youths by accusing them of being “Yahoo Boys”, which basically means internet scammers, they target people who have iPhones, laptops, nice cars, dreadlocks, and so on. Not only do SARS officials profile the Nigerian youths for having basic amenities, but they are also harassed, assaulted, threatened, robbed, and sometimes even shot dead because they refuse to give their belongings to the officials.
This is a very important topic because a lot of innocent Nigerians are being murdered in broad daylight for refusing to hand over their belongings to corrupt police officers.
The Chief of Police in Nigeria stated that the SARS branch had been dissolved but the former SARS officers will be redeployed into the police force as a special unit basically creating the whole problem all over again. The Nigerian youths’ protests are trying to pass a message to the government that the entire police force in Nigeria needs to be reformed because there are a lot of corrupt police officers spread all over the force. There have been countless cases of police officers’ misconduct such as, extorting money from random civilians, assaulting civilians, making unlawful arrests, killing innocent civilians, and walking away without being prosecuted. The problems in the police force a rooted from the corrupt government in Nigeria. The police is so corrupt that even in the current protests they have beaten up a lot of innocent civilians, they opened fire at unarmed citizens leaving many injured and dead. Amidst all these events the president of Nigeria, Muhammadu Buhari has not said a word about anything. The problems in the police force are rooted from the corrupt government in Nigeria, the politicians in places of power in Nigeria steal a lot of government funds for themselves part of which is meant for paying the police officers.
In summary Nigerian citizens are asking for a reformation of the police force but the probability of this happening is very low because this isn’t the first time, they have asked for this and the corruption in the government has to go before these changes can be made.

Iron Ladies of Liberia

Date Milestone Country
1938 Date of Birth Liberia
2005 Eleceted president Liberia
2018 End of tenure as President Liberia

This movie elucidates the story of the first female African president, Ellen Johnson Sirleaf. The movie focuses on how she helped Liberia move forward after experiencing miserable times during the tenure of Charles Taylor.
Ellen appointed women in places of high power such as, chief of police and minister of finance. In this film Ellen showed that a woman can do everything a man can do and better.
Ellen inherited a weak economy from Charles Taylor including a lot of debt owed to other countries and a lot of issues in the country which included overcrowded streets, people living in graveyards and owed pensions to the military that fought for and against Charles Taylor. Ellen handled all these problems in a very effective manner from the way she provided a marketing center for the women that set up on the streets to the way she calmed a crowd of ex-military personnel.
The progress that Ellen made with Liberia was very impressive, considering the state Liberia was in when she came into power, even in the face of people who tried to bring her down with false accusation such as Edwin Snowe. Her progress was very much admired by other countries that the United States cancelled the debt that Liberia owed them, and China aided the country in their growth.
In conclusion, Ellen Johnson Sirleaf showed Africa that their stereotypes of women not being able to handle “a man’s job” are false.
